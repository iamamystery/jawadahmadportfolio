Founding a technology company rewires how you think about engineering. Here are the lessons that survived contact with reality.

## Code is a liability, product is the asset

Every line of code is something the company now maintains. The founder's job is to maximize product delivered per line owned — which sometimes means the best engineering decision is not to build.

## Security sells trust, not features

Clients don't buy threat models. They buy the confidence that shipping with you won't become a headline. Security work is trust work, and trust compounds.

## Constraints are the strategy

A two-person company can't out-build anyone. It can out-focus everyone. Phantex's early bet — secure backend systems, nothing else — turned smallness into positioning.

## The roadmap is a hypothesis

Studio → products → platform looks clean in a deck. In practice, every phase teaches you the next one is shaped differently than you assumed. Hold the vision firmly and the plan loosely.
